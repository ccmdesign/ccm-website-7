# The Problem

The International Development Research Centre, here in Canada, asked us to help them communicate their research on the impact of bleeding edge technologies on the developing world. The main challenge we face in this project is the complexity of the research material and findings. Coming up with simple and interesting ways to illustrate these concepts to the general public is super exciting for our team. We are dealing with very new technology and their “not so obvious” application for the development field, such as “how blockchain would be a solution for universal medical records” and the “implications of Artificial Intelligence for government delivery and efficiency”.

# Users & Audience

The main audience are public servants and NGOs employees from developing countries. The public is highly educated and in contact with the best of breed design material.

# Design Process

The very first step of this process was to really understand these complex subjects and create an inventory of the different design pieces we would create for each report. In these two first publications we created infographics to explain the main concept for each one, and several other graphics to help with examples.

The second step in the process was to create an attractive and pleasing design that would invite the public to dive deeper in these themes. These are long reports, both beyond 100 pages. Legibility and visual cleanness were of utmost importance to keep our readers engaged with the content.
